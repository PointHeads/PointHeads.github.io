Booking Hilton Award Stays the Headache-Free Way

Published on January 30, 2021

Hilton has made great upgrades to their award booking tools over the last few years, most notably with the introduction of the Hilton Points Explorer. It is easier than ever to find and book a Hilton Hotel using points. However, the points explorer is not always the right tool to use. Here we guide you through the hassle-free way to book a Hilton Hotel with points, based on the criteria you have for your upcoming trip.

  1. Book with Flexible Dates/Destination: This is where the Points Explorer shines, but only if you know how to navigate the results.
  2. Book a Known Destination with Strict Dates: This is where the Points Explorer struggles, but a small twist on the normal booking process makes it straightfoward.
  3. Evaluating the Award Booking: Hilton’s Points & Money slider makes it easy to evaluate your award booking once you’ve selected a room.

Flexible Dates/Destination

The Points Explorer is an ideal place to go only under the following conditions:

  1. The dates are flexible.
  2. You have a rough idea of which hotels/brands you might be interested in.

This is because Points Explorer does not have a “date” filter and forces you to select a hotel using only its name, location and mininum/maximum points per night. If you need to explore some hotels first, it is best to go explore somewhere else and come back with some hotel names and/or brands in mind1. When you are ready, let Points Explorer find you the best award options.

  1. Go to Hilton Points Explorer
  2. Set your points per night limit and click Search (yes, leave the “Where do you want to go” input blank, we’ll update that next!)
  3. On the new page, you can dynamically change the “Location” input to filter results. Location can be a Hotel Name, Brand, and/or City/State/Country. There is a lot of flexibility here.
  4. Find a hotel that interests you, and take note of the points range.
  5. Click on Check Availability to open a new page to book for that hotel.
  6. You will notice that the default dates are set to today, and the points per night may be very different than the ones advertised. This is where date flexibility is important.
  7. On Edit stay, click on one of the dates, and select Shop by Price at the bottom of the popup.
  8. Now you can see the points per night for all dates in a month. Navigate around to find a range of dates with the point values you noted earlier. Use this to define the start and end date of your vacation!
    1. If you don’t like any of the date options, just close the window. The Points Explorer is waiting right where you left it on step 4.
  9. Select the first date of your vacation to go back to the Select a Room page.
  10. On Edit stay, click on the second date (departure) and set it to the end date of your vacation.
  11. Select Update to see the new rates for your stay.
  12. Now it’s time to evaluate and finalize the award booking

Strict Dates

When travel dates are strict, Points Explorer is more of a distraction than benefit. In these cases, it is better to use the “Special Rates” feature to find award rates via the normal booking process.

  1. Log in to Hilton.com
  2. Enter the destination and dates of your trip on the main page.
  3. The Minor Twist: Click on Special Rates and select Use Points
  4. Click Find a Hotel to see the options.
  5. The results will appear with the cost listed as points/night. Find a hotel of interest and select View Rates.
  6. Now it’s time to evaluate and finalize the award booking

Evaluating the Award Booking

Once you reach the reservation page of an award booking you can start evaluating whether this is a good point value for you2. The page shows the different room types for the selected hotel, each with points per night (e.g. 26,000 points/night) and approximate dollar value (e.g. Rates From $141).

Select the Book Now box (the one with the present) on the room of interest to get to the final pricing information and reach Hilton’s other useful award booking tool: the Points & Money slider.

On the Payment and Guest Details page, you can use the Points & Money slider to determine if this booking is a good deal. Slide all the way to the right to see the total number of points for the booking (e.g. 104,000 points for a 5 night stay with 26,000 points/night – 5th night free!). Now slide all the way to the left to see the total number of dollars for the booking (e.g. $809.58 for a 5 night stay at $141/night + tax).

Calculate the value of your points: $809.58 / 104,000 * 100 = 0.78 cents/point. And the fifth night free makes this a great award booking3! Congratulations if you found a similar deal.

If you do not have enough points for the full stay, you can always adjust the slider and perform similar calculations to see if it is worth using partial points on the booking. For example, sliding to the middle might give you 52,000 Points + $424.32. Now subtract that cost from the cost of using no points ($809.58) and divide by the points: ($809.58 - $492.14) / 52,000 * 100 = 0.61 cents/point. Not as great as if you used only points, but still a solid use of points for most people.

  1. Hilton doesn’t have a great service for just exploring. Google is your friend here. To dream big, start with “Best Hilton Honors Redemptions” and see where it takes you. Then come back to book once you have some ideas in mind. ↩

  2. If you have any Hilton Elite Status, remember the 5th night is always free for Standard Room Rewards! But this discount is not represented in the points per night estimations until you reach the Payment and Guest Details page of the booking process. ↩

  3. Most evaluations of Hilton points place them between 0.4 and 0.6 cents per point, so receiving 0.78 cents per point would be a great deal for almost everyone. ↩
